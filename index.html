The Louvre's collections hold masterpieces of art from different civilisations, cultures and eras. There are about 300,000 objects in the museum, of which only 35,000 are exhibited in the halls of the Louvre.

The Louvre's exhibits are categorised into the following collections:

Ancient East. The Oriental collection contains more than 100,000 objects of art from the ancient states of the Middle East and the Middle Ages. The department's collection covers the historical period from the Neolithic era to the beginning of Islamic expansion. (Interfluve, Iran, Eastern Mediterranean Sea (Syria, Palestine, Cyprus))
Ancient Greece, Etruria, Rome - one of the first two departments of the museum opened in 1793.(Archaic Greece, Classical Greece, Hellenistic Greece, Etruria, Ancient Rome)
The Art of Islam. The section of Muslim art has existed in the Louvre's Department of Objects of Art since 1893. In 1932, a new Department of Asian Art was created to which all the works of Islamic civilisation of the Louvre are transferred.
Ancient Egypt. The Department of Ancient Egypt was established on 15 May 1826 by order of Charles X. (Roman Egypt, Coptic Egypt; thematic exposition; chronological exposition.)
